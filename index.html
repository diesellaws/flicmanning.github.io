Hi, I'm Flic.

At a young age, I was dancing professionally while managing chronic, invisible illness behind the scenes. Pain was constant. So was pressure. But I refused to shrink. I knew there had to be a way to live fully and work powerfully without pretending my obstacles didn’t exist.

I didn’t have a roadmap, so I started building one.

Step by step, I studied how the body and brain respond to pressure, change, and expectation. I learned how neuroplasticity, wellbeing, and lived experience could be more than personal tools. Over time I began to see that what helped me survive could help all of us thrive.

I studied to became a national radio host and interviewer, speaking with leading figures across media, business, and mental health. Those experiences taught me how to hold conversations that resonate and move people to act.

My memoir, Living Human, is a raw and practical account of that journey. It weaves together my lived experience with tools anyone can use to meet life’s challenges and come out clearer, more grounded, and more themselves.

Today, I speak, teach, and advise on how to design workplaces where people perform at their best by feeling supported, recognised, and empowered. Through keynotes, strategic sessions, and story-led insight, I give teams and individuals practical ways to shift from surviving to growing - at work and in life.

When we embrace people for who they are, we begin to see what’s really possible.

Support, Recognise, and Empower people to succeed on their own terms

1

Identify Support

Every workplace sends a message about who belongs and who must adapt. From policy to culture, people are constantly interpreting whether their needs are respected. When support is inconsistent, trust begins to erode. Understanding how people are supported across your organisation is the first step.

2

Expand Recognition

Recognition shapes who feels seen, who takes up space, and who gets to lead. When achievement is measured by visibility or volume, meaningful contributions are missed. People bring value in many forms, and recognition must reflect that range. Expanding how and where recognition is given is the next step.

3

Amplify Empowerment

Empowerment happens when people are trusted to lead, not just follow. It requires clear access to growth, responsibility, and influence. Without it, people disengage or stay silent. Creating the conditions for everyone to contribute fully is the final step.
